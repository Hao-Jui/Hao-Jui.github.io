About me

During 2017-2019, I was in the group of Chao-Qiang Geng in Hsinchu, Taiwan, with my research focusing on the numerical study of core collapse in alternative gravitational theories. Subsequently, I opted to joint Kostas Kokkotas' group in Tübingen, Germany, where I work on the intimacy between stellar quasi-normal modes (QNMs) with the gravitational/electromagnetic radiations from coalescing binaries in which magnetized neutron star(s) is involved. I also continue the efforts looking at the compact objects in alternative theories with an emphathise on evolutionary simulation of (de)scalarization.

Research

1. Evolutionary study of the pre-merger dynamics of magnetized neutron stars

There are a few percursor flares detected for SGRBs, which may be related to the tidal activities in the pre-merger stage of coalescing binaries. Matching the time of their occurence, resonant g-modes warrant further investigation. We have managed to address the context in the precision of up to 3 PN order effects in orbitial evolution with realistic EOS for single component (fluid) NSs.

2. Dynamical aspects of compact objects in alternative theories

Augmented with some scalar fields as mediators of gravity already at the level of action, scalarized, stationary solutions exist for certain range of central energy density. We first identified the stable branch of spherically-symmetric configuration against fluctuations of its own accord in a perturbative point of view. We then proceed to see that the non-linear effects do not alter the conclusion reached already under lead-order approximation.
