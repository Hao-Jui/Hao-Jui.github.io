About me

I come from Hsinchu -- a county in the North Taiwan, where I spent my young ages and completed my Bachelor degree with double majors in Physics and Mathemactics. Following my academic training at the BCs level, I joined the group of Prof. Chao-Qiang Geng in Hsinchu as a PhD student with my research focusing on the numerical study of core collapse in alternative gravitational theories. In the late 2019, I opted to seek a joint PhD degree between Nat'l Tsing Hua Uni. (Taiwan) and Uni. of Tübingen (Germany), and entered Prof. Kostas Kokkotas' group in Tübingen. Since then, I work mainly on conneting stellar quasi-normal modes (QNMs) to the gravitational/electromagnetic radiations from coalescing binaries, in which magnetized neutron star(s) is involved. I also continue the efforts looking at the compact objects in alternative theories with an emphathise on evolutionary simulation of (de)scalarization.

Research

1. Evolutionary study of the pre-merger dynamics of magnetized neutron stars

There are a few percursor flares detected for SGRBs, which may be related to the tidal activities in the pre-merger stage of coalescing binaries. Matching the time of their occurence, modes with frequencies in the g-mode range, i.e., $\sim$ 100 Hz, warrant further investigation. We have managed to address the scanerio at the precision of up to 3 PN order effects in the orbitial evolution with realistic EOS for single component (fluid) NSs.

2. Dynamical aspects of compact objects in alternative theories

Augmented with some scalar fields as mediators of gravity already at the level of action, scalarized, stationary solutions exist for certain range of central energy density. We first identified the stable branch of spherically-symmetric configuration against fluctuations of its own accord in a perturbative point of view. We then proceed to see that the non-linear effects do not alter the conclusion reached already under lead-order approximation.
